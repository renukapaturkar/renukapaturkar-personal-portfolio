The Start

January 12, 2020
"Being a developer is not knowing everything there is to know in tech, but it is the confidence to say I will figure it out."

It's Tuesday today. I am here sitting on my chair, writing this article instead of doing office work. If you think I have a great job that lets me write in the middle of office hours, you're wrong. I have a desk job, believe me, it's worse than I thought. So I took a day off out of nowhere with my leave balance diminishing with each passing day.

So for an introduction, I am a CS graduate. So I got out of college and I got a job like every graduate I took the job without thinking much. All I was thinking was the financial independence I was about to get. I was happy and then I was in a project completely unrelated to what I thought. So I asked around and there is no way out of it because of the agreement I made with the organization. believe me, I put a lot of faith in my luck, turned out that I am completely out of luck.

Coding wasn't much fun in college because there were people around me setting up new records around it, building cool stuff, and getting appreciation for competitive coding while I struggled to get my head around concepts. I gave up on coding because there is no way I'll be that good.

So when I started my job I realized that I may not all the knowledge in the world about programming but I developed a mindset of a programmer over the year. The mindset to figure out things, to create, to make them work. So here I am on my journey to become a developer and hopefully a good one!
